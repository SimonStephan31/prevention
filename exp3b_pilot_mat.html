Experiment 3b Pilot Study



1 Summary

The pilot study was largely identical to the main study.

1.1 Participants

Sixty subjects (\(M_{age}\,= 39.62\) years, Age range~\(= 21 - 69\) years, 35 female, 25 male) recruited via Prolific () participated in this online study and provided valid data. The inclusion criteria were the same as in the previous experiments. Prolific workers who took part in other studies of this project were excluded from participation. As this is the first study testing the hypothesis, we had no precise information about possible effect sizes but in light of the clear results of Experiment~3a expected a pronounced effect. We thus decided to stop collecting data when 30 subjects in each of two theoretically relevant between-subjects conditions completed the experiment and provided valid data. Subjects received a monetary compensation for their participation.

1.2 Results

Subjects’ singular prevention judgments are shown in the Figure below. As predicted, most subjects (83%, 95% CI [\(0.66, 0.93\)]) said that only the first preventer was the singular preventer of the effect in the condition in which the preventive mechanism variable represented a short-lived event that was contiguous on the “arrival” of a first influence. A one-sample proportions test with continuity correction confirmed that this proportion was significantly different from chance (\(p_{chance}~= 1/3\)), \(\chi^2 (1)~= 31.54\), \(p~< .001\). Also as predicted, subjects’ gave different singular prevention judgments in the condition in which the preventive mechanism variable represented a longer lasting “preparational state” and carried out its action after the process of the second preventer had “arrived”. In line with our hypothesis that people in this case have the impression that the the second preventer also exerted an influence, the majority of subjects (77%, 95% CI [\(0.59, 0.88\)]) said that both preventers were singular preventers of the effect. A one-sample proportions test with continuity correction confirmed that this proportion was significantly different from chance (\(p_{chance}~= 1/3\)), \(\chi^2 (1)~= 23.44\), \(p~< .001\). Furthermore, a two-sample test for equality of proportions with continuity correction confirmed that the proportion of subjects saying that only the first preventer was the singular preventer of the effect was significantly higher in the contiguous preventive mechanism condition than in the delayed preventive mechanism condition, \(\chi^2 (1)~= 19.35\), \(p~< .001\). Also, a second a two-sample test for equality of proportions with continuity correction confirmed that the proportion of subjects saying that both preventers were singular preventers of the effect was significantly higher in the delayed preventive mechanism condition than in the contiguous preventive mechanism condition, \(\chi^2 (1)~= 19.353\), \(p~< .001\).

5.1 Data set information

The data set contains the following columns:

  • run_id: a subject ID
  • condition_1: factor coding the in total four conditions (whether the mechanism variable is delayed or not: 01 and 02 vs. 03 and 04). It also codes the counterbalancing factor, which was whether the first preventer that occurred in the test case was the upper (01 and 03) or the lower (02 and 04).
  • test_case: factor coding the type of situation (whether the mechanism variable is delayed or not).
  • first_preventer: factor coding the order in which the preventers occurred (counterbalancing factor).
  • sat_selection_upper_demo: answer to a check query ensuring that subjects correlct understood what happened in the first learning animation (where only the upper preventer acted).
  • sat_selection_lower_demo: answer to a check query ensuring that subjects correlct understood what happened in the first learning animation (where only the lower preventer acted)
  • sat_selection_test: Main DV; subjects singular prevention judgment.
  • explanation: subjects’ explanations of their judgments.
  • preventers_check: subjects’ answers to the learning check question about which preventer reacted first.
  • selection: subjects’ singular prevention judgments (they selected one of three options; first vs. second vs. both)
  • desktop_conf: subjects had to confirm that they take part via a Laptop or Desktop PC (only the data of subjects who confirmed were registered)
  • attent_conf: subjects’ confirmation to pay attention (only the data of subjects who confirmed were registered)
  • age: subjects’ age (in years)
  • gender: 1 = male, 2 = female, 3 = non-binary
  • recorded_at: date and time per subject
  • main_dv: a numeric version of the “preventer_selection” column
